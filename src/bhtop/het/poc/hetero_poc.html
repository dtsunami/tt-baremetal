01

The thesis

Tenstorrent's own stack leaves the x280 idle (a silicon-bug workaround) and its JIT won't let the two engines share memory. This POC does the opposite: it extracts the launch and NoC primitives from tt-metal into a lean bare-metal harness, then puts both engines to work on one real workload — 3D Gaussian splatting — with no host arithmetic in the render loop.

04

The trick that made the composite fit

serial scan → matmul

Front-to-back alpha compositing is a serial recurrence — the thing vector hardware hates. Reformulated in log-space it becomes matmuls with constant matrices, so it runs on the engines already proven: T = exp( log(1−α) @ S▲ ), and one triangular-plus-identity matrix folds the prefix-sum and the per-splat weight into a single pass. Opacity is a diagonal matmul; the −½·Σv² is a pair-sum matmul. No new kernel type — just MVMUL + SFPU.

Honest edges — what turns the POC into the product

x280 owns projection too
Sort, gather, and operand layout are on the x280 now; the one-time whitening still runs host-side and hands bf16 coeffs over. Moving it on-chip needs the x280 scalar FPU enabled.
The backward pass
Forward is complete on the heterogeneous machine; the gradient scatter-add is the x280's next natural job.
The number
Correctness first, by design. A measured head-to-head vs the host-orchestrated path needs the sort and stream at full scale.
Fleet at scale
16 cores streaming into the ring, and the x280 seize has a reset-once quirk to work around for a clean 16/16.
